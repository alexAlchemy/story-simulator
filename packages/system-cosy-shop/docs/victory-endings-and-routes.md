# Victory, Endings & Aspirational Routes

Cosy shop progression should distinguish between **winning the game** and
**becoming a particular kind of shopkeeper**.

The baseline victory condition can be relatively approachable: keep the shop open,
pay the major debt, survive the season, pass a final inspection, or make it
through the town crisis. A player with moderate understanding of the mechanics
should be able to clear this bar.

The deeper replay goal is different:

> You won, but what kind of life did you build?

## Design Terms

- **Victory condition:** the baseline requirement for a successful run.
- **Ending:** the narrative outcome shown at the end of the game.
- **Outcome axis:** a measured dimension that shapes endings, such as wealth,
  goodwill, craft mastery, apprentice growth, reputation, relationships, or
  wellbeing.
- **Aspirational route:** an optional high-level goal or identity the player may
  pursue across the run.
- **Strategic tradeoff:** a design tension where pursuing one route naturally
  makes another harder.
- **Mastery challenge:** a difficult combined goal that skilled players can chase
  after understanding the system.

These terms are not rigid categories, but they help separate the layers of play.

## Example Routes

Possible aspirational routes for a cosy potion shop game:

- **Mega Merchant:** build wealth, upgrade aggressively, dominate supply,
  negotiate hard, and become commercially powerful.
- **Local Saint:** give generously, forgive debts, protect vulnerable customers,
  build goodwill, and become beloved by the town.
- **Potions Master:** pursue rare recipes, high quality, experimentation,
  difficult commissions, and craft excellence.
- **Trusted Mentor:** raise the apprentice into confidence, independence, and
  skill.
- **Discreet Confidant:** become the shop people trust with secrets, shame,
  dangerous needs, and private remedies.
- **Guild Darling:** earn legitimacy, pass inspections, cultivate professional
  standing, and gain institutional support.
- **Dangerous Genius:** create powerful or unstable work, attract risky clients,
  and push craft beyond safe limits.

Routes can produce named endings, influence ending text, unlock late scenes, or
act as achievement-style goals.

## Tradeoffs

Aspirational routes are strongest when they pull against each other.

Examples:

- Giving potions away supports **Local Saint**, but weakens **Mega Merchant**.
- Charging premium prices supports **Mega Merchant**, but may damage goodwill.
- Risky experiments support **Potions Master** or **Dangerous Genius**, but can
  raise gossip heat or cause accidents.
- Training the apprentice supports **Trusted Mentor**, but costs time, patience,
  ingredients, and short-term efficiency.
- Discretion can build intimate trust, but may conflict with guild reporting or
  public reputation.

The goal is not to make routes mutually exclusive. The goal is to make combined
success meaningful. A skilled player might become both wealthy and beloved, but
that should require planning, sacrifice, and clever use of opportunities.

## Victory vs Ending

The game can have a simple success check and a richer ending evaluation.

Example:

- **Victory:** the shop survives rent day and remains open.
- **Ending texture:** the shop is rich but cold, poor but beloved, renowned for
  mastery, feared for dangerous cures, held together by apprentice loyalty, or
  quietly trusted by those with nowhere else to go.

This lets the game support accessible completion while preserving replay depth.
Players can first learn how to survive, then return to chase a more specific
identity or harder combined outcome.

## Design Test

When adding a major mechanic or scene, ask:

- Which outcome axes can this affect?
- Which aspirational routes does it support or complicate?
- Does it help the player pursue a kind of shopkeeper, not just a bigger number?
- Does it create a meaningful tradeoff between routes?
- Could this matter in the ending?

For `system-cosy-shop`, the core question is not only "Did you win?" It is:

> What did success cost, and what did the shop become known for?
